COVID-19 Is Not A Vacations

Nowadays the world is suffering in an infectious disease caused by a new virus which is Coronavirus Diseases (COVID-19). This disease attacks our respiratory system (like the flu), the symptoms of COVID-19 are cough, fever, tiredness, and in a few cases, patients have to face low breathing problems. People may be sick with this virus in 1 to 14 days before developing these symptoms, and about 80% of people get rid of it without taking any special treatment but, rarely this disease may be serious or can be fatal.


How COVID-19 Comes?

It's not come from the sky to the earth this virus also has its ancestors it is a subfamily of Orthocoronavirinae and there are many more like this. The first time it is discovered in 1960 in two humans and a chicken. Other members of this family have since been identified, including SARS-CoV in 2003, HCoV NL63 in 2004, HKU1 in 2005, MERS-CoV in 2012, and SARS-CoV-2 (formerly known as 2019-nCoV) in 2019. Most of these have involved serious respiratory tract infections.


How COVID-19 Spread?

This virus spreads through contact of bodies, whenever we shake hands and hug someone who suffers from fever this virus can transfer to the other person or through cough or sneeze. It also spread when you touch an object which has a virus so be careful to do this kind of activity.

What We Do To Prevent COVID-19?


Prevention Is Better Than Cure
But there's no vaccine made to prevent this virus, but we can protect ourself and help prevent spreading the virus to others if you:

Do

  • Wash your hands regularly for 20 seconds, with soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub.
  • Cover your nose and mouth with a disposable tissue or flexed elbow when you cough or sneeze.
  • Avoid close contact (1 meter or 3 feet) with people who are unwell.
  • Stay home and self-isolate from others in the household if you feel unwell.

Don't

  • Touch your eyes, nose, or mouth if your hands are not clean.
